## Key Content Extracted

### The Origin Story

**Title**: "Cómo empezó la historia..."

**Summary**: In summer 1987, Ernesto Acher reconnected with Jorge Navarro and proposed forming a group. Starting with Navarro's existing trio (Juan Amaral on bass, Pocho Lapouble on drums), they added Carlos Costantini (trumpet), Juan Carlos Bazán (clarinet), Hugo Pierre (saxophones), Ricardo Lew (guitar), and Ernesto on trombone.

After early departures by Bazán and Lapouble, Enrique Varela (saxophones) and Zurdo Roizner (drums) joined. The eight members rehearsed intensively for six months in early 1988 (9am-1pm daily) without a single absence.

Originally conceived as a dixieland-style band with variations, their versatility grew to encompass jazz, tango, folklore, and salsa. A chance meeting with Alfredo Zemma, director of Teatro Nacional Cervantes, led to an unexpected opportunity: a premiere on June 18, 1988, in prime time at the prestigious venue - despite the band not even having a name yet!

With support from José Tarica (rehearsal space) and Armando Rapallo (journalist, Clarín), their debut night sold out completely. Thus began a project that, while shorter-lived than deserved, holds a special place in the hearts of all involved.
